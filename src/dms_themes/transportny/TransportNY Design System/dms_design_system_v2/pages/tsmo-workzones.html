Work Zones.

Construction is New York's largest source of attributed delay — 188.6M vehicle-hours since 2018, twenty-two times the delay attributed to crashes. This page tracks where that delay happens, how long work zones run, and how much of it is planned work versus emergency response.

Planned construction drives nearly all work-zone delay — which means most of it is schedulable, and mitigable through lane-closure timing and corridor coordination.

Method. Work-zone events are TRANSCOM Construction, Maintenance, and Emergency Operations categories, NY-filtered. Delay is attributed per event by joining its affected TMCs against NPMRDS probe speeds and subtracting the recurrent baseline; the share-of-total compares against the statewide excessive-delay attribution (2019–2020 not computed). Dollars apply FHWA's per-person-hour value of time. Full definitions, formulas, and caveats: Data & Methodology.
