// the nysdot tsmo program

Operating what we own, better.

New York's highway network is largely built. The biggest near-term gains now come from operating it well — and from measuring whether we do. In 2024 New Yorkers lost 223 million vehicle-hours to excessive delay; 47% of it was non-recurrent — crashes, work zones, weather. That is the share operations can move, and it is what this section of TransportNY tracks.

// 01

What is TSMO

Transportation Systems Management & Operations is a family of strategies for getting the most out of infrastructure that already exists — clearing crashes faster, coordinating work zones, timing signals, informing travelers — rather than relying on new capacity alone. FHWA's Capability Maturity Model assesses agency operations across six dimensions; this site is the Performance Measurement dimension made public.

Business Processes

Planning & programming that treat operations as a core investment

Systems & Tech

ITS devices, traffic management centers & the data platforms behind them

Performance Measurement

Define measures, collect data, manage by it — these dashboards

Culture

Leadership & agency-wide understanding of the value of operations

Organization & Workforce

Staff capacity, structure & development for an operations mission

Collaboration

Working across agencies — TRANSCOM, MPOs, responders, FHWA

// 02

The NYSDOT program

NYSDOT's TSMO Strategic Plan commits the Department to managing the existing system as deliberately as it builds new assets — and to enterprise performance reporting so the public can see how operations is doing. The Department operates through eleven regions, each with traffic management capability, HELP service patrols on major corridors, and a statewide partnership with TRANSCOM for real-time event data. These dashboards are the reporting commitment delivered: one statewide picture, drillable to every region and corridor.
