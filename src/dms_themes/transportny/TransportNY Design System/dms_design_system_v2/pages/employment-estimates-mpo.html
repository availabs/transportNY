Which geography level should you model on?

All three levels are deterministic aggregations of the same block-level estimate, so they're mutually consistent — pick the level that matches your TAZ granularity and tolerance for noise.

Mutual consistency. The block-group totals are deterministic sums of constituent block estimates; tract totals are deterministic sums of block-group totals. IPF runs once at the block level — coarser geographies are pure aggregation. So you can mix-and-match levels in one model without double-counting.

// 06 travel-demand modeling recipe

Plugging this into your 4-step / activity-based model.

1
Attractions by purpose

Multiply est_emp_naics at block-group by NCHRP 716 / your local trip rates per industry.

A_HBW(BG) = Σ_NAICS est_emp · t_HBW_NAICS
A_HBO(BG) = total_emp · 0.35
A_NHB(BG) = retail_emp · 1.8 + svc_emp · 1.1
2
Cross-check with LEHD RAC

LEHD RAC is workers' residence side. Use it for HBW productions; the estimate is HBW attractions. Both are anchored to the same QCEW universe → home↔work flows balance after gravity step.

P_HBW(BG_home) = LEHD RAC
A_HBW(BG_work) = est_emp · rate
3
Mode choice / transit catchment

Block-level est_emp within a ½-mile walk shed of each transit stop is the catchment denominator. Anchor blocks (hospitals, schools) usually dominate — verify your stop placement covers them.

catchment(stop) = Σ est_emp · walk_distance ≤ 0.5 mi
4
Sensitivity test

Filter data_confidence ≥ medium and re-run. If your AM peak hour assignment moves > 5% on key screenlines, the low-conf tail matters for your network. File anchor-catalog candidates for the affected blocks.

∆v/c at screenline < 5% → trust full
∆ > 5% → escalate to research team
