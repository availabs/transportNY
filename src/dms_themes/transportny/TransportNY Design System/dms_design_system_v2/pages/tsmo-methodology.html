Data & Methodology.

How every number on these dashboards is made. Four sources, six measures, one rule: every panel states its window, its method, and its coverage — and where the data is thin, we say so instead of smoothing it over.

Source catalog

NPMRDS probe speeds
FHWA probe feed · ground truth

One observed travel time per TMC per 5 minutes — all vehicles, passenger, and freight. Everything else on this site derives from it.

54,249 TMCs 14.0B 5-min records 2017 → May 2026 lag ~2 weeks
TRANSCOM events
coalition feed · the event store

Incidents, construction, conditions, and special events from the TRANSCOM coalition. The feed covers the whole multi-state region — every page here filters to New York.

3.7M events 2015 → present live feed multi-state · NY-filtered

excessive_delay · derived in-house

Our TMC × month delay table — replays speeds around events to split delay into cause buckets. The engine behind the Congestion and Work Zones dashboards.

MAP-21 · annual

The federally defined reliability measures, computed per TMC per year from the probe feed. The Reliability dashboard reports these exactly as FHWA defines them.

// 02

Measure definitions

One measure, one explainer. Each card is the canonical method note that every dashboard panel links back to.

Excessive delay delay attribution veh-hrs

Time spent traveling slower than the excessive-delay threshold — the greater of 20 mph or 60% of the posted speed — multiplied by the vehicles present in that 5-minute slice. Summed to TMC × month, then split into cause buckets by overlapping TRANSCOM events.

Caveat: 2019–2020 were not computed in the current run; 2025 is partial through October. Trend charts show the gap rather than interpolating.

threshold = max(20 mph, 0.6 × posted)
delay₅ₘᵢₙ  = max(0, TTobs − TTthr) × veh₅ₘᵢₙ
veh-hrs    = Σ delay₅ₘᵢₙ   per TMC × month
recurrent  = total − non-recurrent
Congestion cost delay attribution + AADT $

Delay monetized. Vehicle-hours become person-hours through an average occupancy of 1.7 persons per vehicle, then dollars through the FHWA value-of-time factor. Truck shares use the AADT single/combination splits at a separate freight rate.

Dollar factors are fixed per analysis year and stated on every $ panel.

cost = delay_vh × 1.7 occ/veh
            × $ per person-hr (FHWA)
     + truck_vh × $ per truck-hr
LOTTR · level of travel time reliability PM3 ratio

How much worse a bad day is than a normal one: the 80th-percentile travel time divided by the 50th, computed for four day-parts. A segment is reliable when every period stays under 1.5. Reported as the % of person-miles on reliable segments.

LOTTRₚ = TT₈₀ₜₕ ÷ TT₅₀ₜₕ
reliable ⇔ LOTTRₚ < 1.5  ∀ 4 periods
report  = % person-miles reliable
TTTR · truck travel time reliability PM3 index

The freight version, interstates only: 95th-percentile truck travel time over the 50th, across five periods (the four LOTTR day-parts plus overnight). The statewide index is the length-weighted average of each segment's worst period.

TTTRₚ = TT₉₅ₜₕ(truck) ÷ TT₅₀ₜₕ(truck)
index = Σ len × max(TTTRₚ) ÷ Σ len
PHED · peak-hour excessive delay PM3 hrs / capita

Annual person-hours of excessive delay during peak periods (6–10 AM, 3–7 PM weekdays), per capita, for each large urbanized area. Same speed threshold as excessive delay; population from the Census UZA estimate.

PHED = Σ peak delay × occupancy
       ÷ UZA population   (per year)
Roadway clearance time TRANSCOM · TIM coverage-badged

Time from incident verification to all lanes reopened — the FHWA TIM headline measure. We compute it only on the timestamped subset: lanes-open exists on 42.7% of events and verification on 20.1%, so every clearance figure carries a "based on N% of incidents" badge — and we trend the coverage itself.

Limitation: medians on a sparse subset can shift as coverage improves. Threshold shares (% cleared < 90 min) tolerate the missingness better than means — that's what the Incidents dashboard headlines.

RCT = end_all_lanes_open
    − incident_verified
subset: both timestamps present
report: median · 85th · % < 90 min
        + coverage %

Glossary

TMCTraffic Message Channel segment — the ½–2 mile road piece every probe record keys on. New York's network has 54,249 of them.
LOTTRLevel of Travel Time Reliability — 80th ÷ 50th percentile travel time. A segment is reliable when every day-part stays under 1.5.
TTTRTruck Travel Time Reliability — 95th ÷ 50th percentile truck travel time, interstates only.
PHEDPeak Hour Excessive Delay — annual person-hours of excessive delay per capita in large urbanized areas.
vehicle-hoursOne vehicle delayed one hour = 1 veh-hr. The common unit behind every delay figure on the site; person-hours = veh-hrs × occupancy.
f_systemFHWA functional classification, 1 (interstate) through 7 (local). f_system 0 marks our extended set of local and unclassified roads.
NYSDOT regionThe eleven administrative regions (R1 Capital District … R11 New York City) every dashboard's region selector rolls up to.
