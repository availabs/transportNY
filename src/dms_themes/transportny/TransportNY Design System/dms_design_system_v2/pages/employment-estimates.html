// 02 universe coverage · who's counted where

// 04 cell confidence · two-axis labelling

Knowing which (block, NAICS) cells to trust as model inputs.

data_confidence · 5 tiers · Phase 3 rework

How well do the sources agree at this cell?

Binned product of three axes: agreement (BP × WAC × LODES sign + magnitude), support (count of sources reporting ≥1 job), and a magnitude modifier (tiny cells get a penalty so noise doesn't masquerade as high confidence).

very_highAll 3 sources align ± 20%, cell ≥ 50 jobs25.0%
high2 of 3 sources align ± 30%29.8%
mediumTwo sources nonzero, modest gap18.1%
lowOnly 1 source reporting, or large disagreement10.7%
very_lowTiny cell (≤ 5 jobs) + sparse signal16.3%

~55% of cells are high+, and the small low tail concentrates in tiny-employment cells — see the employment-weighted reframe below.

bp_trust · 4 tiers · Phase 3

Is the Business Points signal trustworthy here?

Per (block, NAICS): anomaly rate + geocode quality + ZBP cross-match + sample size. Feeds the row-α blend — high bp_trust → BP gets more weight, low bp_trust → WAC gets more weight.

highRooftop geocode, clean anomaly, ZBP confirms41.0%
mediumMost signals positive, mild anomaly flag24.2%
lowAnomaly, small N, or geocode quality below threshold21.7%
noneNo BP record at this cell — WAC-only signal13.1%

For modeling: filter data_confidence ≥ medium for primary inputs (covers 72.9% of cells, 94.3% of employment); review low / very_low cells in your TAZs by hand.

cell count vs employment-weighted

Both views matter, and they tell the same story from different angles. 54.8% of cells are high+. Re-weight those same cells by the employment they carry and the picture sharpens — 90.3% of NY employment lives in high+ cells:

  • • By cell count (385k cells): 25% very_high · 30% high · 18% medium · 11% low · 16% very_low
  • • By employment captured (8.47M jobs): 40% very_high · 50% high · 4% medium · 2% low · 4% very_low

The very_low tier is 16% of cells but only ~4% of jobs — tiny-employer blocks where source disagreement is statistical noise. For TAZ trip generation in your model, the high+ majority is what matters.

Legacy column: est_confidence

The published rows also carry a v0-era est_confidence column (3-tier: high · medium · low) emitted for back-compat. Its distribution is the much-less-flattering 9.2% high · 8.8% medium · 81.5% low because it doesn't carry the magnitude penalty or the agreement decomposition. // 05 anchor catalog · curated overrides

// 07 for transportation planners

How to plug these into your travel-demand model.

Trip-generation rates need stable, industry-resolved employment at modeling geography. These three recipes give you a starting point; the MPO+block page drills further.

step 1
Trip generation

Use block_group × NAICS as the unit for industry-specific trip rates. Block-level is finer than most TAZ systems need and amplifies the 84.9% low-conf tail.

employment_by_industry_blockgroup_2022.gpkg
→ keep est_emp, weight by NAICS-specific trip rate
step 2
HBW productions / attractions

Pair with LEHD RAC (workers' residence side) for the production end. The estimates are the attraction end. Both are reconciled to the same QCEW universe so the home↔work flows balance.

attractions = Σ(est_emp × HBW_rate_NAICS)
productions = LEHD RAC by workers
step 3
Sensitivity test the low tail

Run your model twice: once with the full estimate, once filtering data_confidence ≥ medium. If your output trip distribution moves > 5%, the low-conf tail matters and you need anchor catalog expansion.

diff < 5% → trust full estimate
diff > 5% → flag NAICS, file expansion ticket
// 08 pipeline history · v0 → v5 roadmap

Five iterations, each fixing what the last got wrong.

  1. v0 · single-pass baseline

    QCEW county anchor × within-county BP/WAC blend (heuristic α). No sub-county marginal.

  2. v1 · IPF + ZBP marginal

    Added ZBP × NAICS-2 ZCTA marginal. IPF with heuristic α(NAICS).

  3. v2 · data-driven α

    α(county, NAICS) derived from ZBP shape-match instead of a hand-coded table.

  4. v3 · confidence labels

    data_confidence (5-tier) and bp_trust (4-tier) added as side tables. v2 allocation untouched.

  5. v4 · runaway control · shipped 2026-05-21

    7 fixes for block runaway: P1 overshoot gate · P2 per-cell cap · P3 anchor catalog · P4 ZCTA-targeted IPF damping · P5 row-level α · P6 BP establishment prior · P7 LODES soft constraint.

    Fixed: Manhattan block 360610007001001 (NAICS 62) 103,448 → 6,721 jobs · within 25% of analysis_2's 8,314.

  6. v5 · planned

    Nonemployer Statistics + railroad supplements · pct_q two-sided fix · anchor catalog expansion (SUNY, hospitals, prisons, airports) · CBP LP-imputation · promotion to dms-server dataType.
