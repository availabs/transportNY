Overview.

A plain-language introduction to the National Performance Management Research Data Set and how TransportNY uses it.

What this site is for

Welcome to the documentation section of the National Performance Management Research Data Set Platform. TransportNY is the access layer the New York State Department of Transportation and its MPO partners use to work with NPMRDS data. The platform is free to use, with public-read access on every dashboard and a sign-in flow for saving templates and configuring alerts.

If you're new here, start with getting started to pick a region and run your first report. If you know what you need, jump to MAP-21 PM3 for federal reporting, floating car for route-level speed analysis, or congestion for delay attribution.

What is the NPMRDS?

The National Performance Management Research Data Set is a probe-based travel-time feed maintained by the Federal Highway Administration. It records observed travel times at 5-minute intervals for every TMC segment on the National Highway System and the Truck Parking Facilities network. The data is anonymized and aggregated; individual vehicles are never identifiable.

TransportNY ingests NPMRDS nightly and joins it with NYSDOT's reference data — TMC linework, road class, jurisdiction, ownership — to produce the regional, county, MPO, and corridor views you see in the dashboards. All travel-time numbers you see on this site originate from NPMRDS; congestion-cost dollars are computed using the FHWA cost-of-delay factor.

Strengths and limits

  • Strong on the NHS. Every interstate and most principal arterials are fully covered. Truck Parking Facilities are tracked separately.
  • Weak on local streets. Functional class 6-7 segments may have sparse probe coverage. Don't use NPMRDS for residential street analysis.
  • Probe-based, not count-based. Travel times reflect the experience of a moving vehicle. NPMRDS doesn't tell you the volume of traffic — pair with HPMS AADT or NYSDOT continuous-count-station data for volume.
  • Lagged by 24-72 hours. Yesterday's data is generally available; today's data is not. For real-time, see 511NY.

Pulling data via API

Every report renders against the same Falcor / GraphQL endpoint that powers the dashboards. Sign in to generate an API key; rate limits are 10,000 requests/day for public users and unlimited for NYSDOT partner agencies. A minimal speed-pull looks like this: 

SELECT tmc, AVG(travel_time_sec) AS tt
FROM npmrds.observations
WHERE date BETWEEN '2025-01-01' AND '2025-12-31'
  AND tmc IN ('120N04519', '120N04520')
  AND epoch BETWEEN 24 AND 40  -- 06:00–10:00 AM peak
GROUP BY tmc;

See the API guide for full reference, the data dictionary for column-level docs, and methodology for how reliability indices and cost calculations are computed.
